Registration Advice for Fall 2014

Highlighted FAQs related to Course Offerings

There are 3 offerings that are using Experimental Grading . You may only take 1 of the 3. (Topics in Bioengineering, Computational Signal Processing, Computational Bayesian Statistics)
 
The Olin Self Study (OSS) Requirement can be satisfied by AHSE4190, AHSE4590, in addition to a standard Independent Study Activity that meets the OSS objectives.
 
Courses that satisfy the Probability and Statistics (MTH2130) course requirement. There are two being offered in the fall. If you wish to use the course as your prob/stat requirement, you need to do a CSTB for to inform your degree audit of this choice. You need only the form and your signatures (no others). 
 
Retiring of AHSE1500, The Entrepreneurial Initiative (formerly Foundations of Business and Entrepreneurship):  In addition to piloting the replacement course, AHSE1515: Products and Markets, we are also exploring other options for students to fulfill this requirement.  These options are not yet available.  If you need this requirement but are unable to talke the fall pilot, please stay tuned for other options. 
 
Robotics:  The offering ENGR3599A: Special Topics in Computing, A Computational Introduction to Robotics, allows flexibility in the E: Robotics concentration.  Depending on a student's background, either ENGR3390: Fundamentals of Robotics OR ENGR3599A: Spec Top: A Computational Intro to Robotics may be used as an introductory course.  More information on the next iteration of the E: Robotics concentration will be available in the 2014-15 Catalog.
 
Biology:  Starting this year, if a student took the 2013 AP Biology exam and received a score of a 5, other options to meet the Olin Intro Biology requirement are available.  For the Fall 2014 semester, the Biology Special Topics course, Emerging Technologies in Cancer Research and Treatment is an acceptable alternative.

Schedule Display Update

The student schedule display is term specific.  If your current term is not displayed, select the view details link and choose the appropriate term from drop down menu.   Now select the view your schedule button.
 

Getting this Error? "Unauthorized Course Entry"

If you are getting the error message below when trying to register it could be one of the following:  1) you were logged in before your registration time; or 2) you were pasting course numbers/sections into the add by course code boxes.  You can fix these by refreshing your cache or by logging out/logging back in; AND by not pasting course info into the boxes.  You should be typing them in. 
 

Do you have an Error Message saying HOLD ?

If you are restricted in anyway with an error message regarding a HOLD,  choose personal info link to the right of your name, then the academic info section for information concerning the hold details.
